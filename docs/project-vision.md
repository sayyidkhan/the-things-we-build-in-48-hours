# Project Vision

## Project Name

**The Things We Build in 48 Hours**

## Project Description

A memoir documenting the journey of a builder seeking purpose through one hundred hackathons.

## Core Idea

This is not just a portfolio.

This is a living memoir of a builder who uses hackathons as a way to discover purpose, sharpen identity, and understand what it means to build in the age of AI.

Each hackathon is treated as a chapter:
- the idea
- the people
- the chaos
- the build
- the failure
- the win
- the lesson

The website documents the journey from early experiments to one hundred hackathons.

## Why This Exists

Most portfolios show outcomes.

This project shows the journey.

The goal is to capture the emotional, technical, and personal evolution of a builder who keeps showing up, keeps building, and keeps searching for meaning through creation.

## Positioning

**The Things We Build in 48 Hours** is a memoir-style website about hackathons, AI-assisted creation, rapid prototyping, and personal growth.

It sits between:
- a personal website
- a public journal
- a project archive
- a hackathon memoir
- a builder philosophy

## Target Audience

This site is for:
- builders
- hackers
- students
- AI engineers
- startup founders
- hackathon organisers
- people trying to find purpose through creation

## Website Goals

The site should help visitors understand:

1. Who Sayyid Khan is
2. Why he is doing one hundred hackathons
3. What he has built
4. What he has learned
5. How his philosophy as a builder is evolving

## Content Pillars

### 1. The Memoir

Reflective essays about the journey, identity, ambition, failure, and purpose.

Example topics:
- Why I keep joining hackathons
- What 48 hours can teach you about yourself
- The loneliness of building
- Winning is not the only proof
- Searching for purpose through prototypes

### 2. The Hackathon Archive

A chronological record of all hackathons.

Each entry should include:
- hackathon number
- event name
- year/date
- project name
- team members
- result
- demo link
- GitHub link
- photos/videos
- key lesson

### 3. The Projects

A showcase of products and prototypes built during hackathons.

Each project should include:
- problem statement
- solution
- stack
- what worked
- what failed
- what I would improve
- demo/screenshots

### 4. The Lessons

Practical lessons from repeated building.

Example themes:
- ship early
- deploy before polishing
- AI agents are powerful but dangerous
- judges need to see working software
- clarity beats complexity
- volume negates luck

### 5. The Philosophy

The deeper operating system behind the journey.

Core beliefs:
- Building is a form of self-discovery
- Repetition creates luck
- Shipping reveals truth
- Failure compounds into instinct
- Purpose is discovered through motion

## Tone of Voice

The tone should be:
- cinematic
- honest
- reflective
- simple
- human
- slightly poetic but not overdone

Avoid sounding:
- too corporate
- too startup-bro
- too motivational
- too technical
- too abstract
